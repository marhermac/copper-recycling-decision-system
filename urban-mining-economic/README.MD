# ♻️ Decision Lab – Case Study 01

# Urban Mining Economics

**A Data Analytics Approach to Urban Copper Recovery**

---

## Overview

Urban Mining Economics is the first research case developed under the **Decision Lab** initiative.

The objective is to evaluate the economic viability of recovering copper from discarded household electrical equipment using data analytics and decision science principles.

Instead of focusing only on material recovery, this project transforms operational observations into business decisions supported by analytical models.

---

# Research Question

> Can urban copper recovery become a profitable economic activity through data-driven decision making?

---

# Objectives

- Evaluate copper recovery opportunities.
- Compare different recyclable assets.
- Estimate profitability.
- Calculate Return on Investment (ROI).
- Support investment decisions.
- Demonstrate how Data Analytics can be applied to real-world business problems.

---

# Methodology

The project follows the Decision Lab framework.

1. Observe
2. Collect Data
3. Analyze
4. Compare Alternatives
5. Build Decision Models
6. Recommend Actions

---

# Decision Engine

The application includes a simple Decision Engine capable of:

- Ranking assets
- Comparing ROI
- Estimating profitability
- Selecting the best investment opportunity
- Generating executive recommendations

This architecture will be reused in future Decision Lab projects.
