# Copper Supply Chain Analysis

# Research Question

How does copper move through the urban recycling ecosystem in Argentina?

---

# Stage 1: Urban Recycler

Activities:

* Finds discarded assets.
* Extracts copper-containing components.
* Recovers copper manually.
* Sells recovered copper to local scrap dealers.

Economic function:

Labor-intensive value creation.

---

# Stage 2: Local Scrap Dealer

Activities:

* Purchases copper from multiple recyclers.
* Aggregates inventory.
* Provides immediate liquidity.
* Stores copper temporarily.

Economic function:

Capital provider and aggregation intermediary.

Observed behavior:

* Purchases copper immediately.
* Operates with limited cash availability.
* Accumulates approximately weekly inventory.
* Sells to larger operators.

---

# Stage 3: Wholesale Recycler

Hypothesis:

* Purchases large quantities of copper.
* Sorts and classifies materials.
* Accumulates industrial volumes.
* Sells to processors or foundries.

Economic function:

Scale and logistics optimization.

---

# Stage 4: Industrial Processor

Hypothesis:

* Processes copper scrap.
* Removes impurities.
* Produces industrial-grade copper material.

Economic function:

Material transformation.

---

# Stage 5: Foundry / Export Market

Hypothesis:

* Produces refined copper.
* Supplies industrial markets.
* Participates in international commodity markets.

Economic function:

Final value realization.

---

# Preliminary Supply Chain Model

Urban Recycler
↓
Local Scrap Dealer
↓
Wholesale Recycler
↓
Industrial Processor
↓
Foundry / Export Market

---

# Preliminary Observation

The urban copper economy appears to operate as a multi-stage value chain where each participant captures value through different mechanisms:

* Labor
* Capital
* Aggregation
* Processing
* Industrial transformation
