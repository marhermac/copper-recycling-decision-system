# Preliminary Results

# Urban Mining Economics in Argentina

## Preliminary Findings from Field Observation and Economic Modeling

---

# Executive Summary

This study explores the economic viability of urban copper recovery activities in Argentina through field observation, economic modeling, and data analysis.

The analysis suggests that urban mining can be modeled as a profitable economic activity under certain market conditions.

---

# Methodology

The model was constructed using:

* Direct field observations.
* Estimated copper content per asset.
* Historical copper prices in Argentina.
* Recovery efficiency assumptions.
* Economic modeling techniques.

Assumptions:

* Copper recovery efficiency: 85%.
* Copper price: 15,500 ARS/kg.
* Purchase price per recovered asset: 5,000 ARS.
* Working time: 40 hours per week.

# Key Findings

## Finding 1

Air conditioning units appear to be the highest-value urban mining asset among the analyzed household appliances.

---

## Finding 2

Microwave transformers provide a very high value-to-size ratio and represent an important source of recoverable copper.

---

## Finding 3

Electric motors constitute the primary economic unit of urban copper recovery activities.

---

## Finding 4

Urban mining profitability appears to be driven by a portfolio of assets rather than by a single commodity.

---

# Preliminary Conclusions

The results provide preliminary evidence that:

* Urban mining can be modeled as an economic activity.
* Asset selection significantly impacts profitability.
* Air conditioners and transformers appear to be the most profitable urban mining assets.
* Electric motors represent the core productive unit of urban copper recovery.
* Urban mining activities may operate as a portfolio management strategy involving assets with different risk and profitability profiles.
